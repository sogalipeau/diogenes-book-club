Then Let's Talk!

I can't get over the beauty of fictional worlds. Writers like Isaac Asimov, Frank Herbert, and J. R. R. Tolkien inspired and drove my thirst for reading. If there's one surefire way to get me to talk, it's going to be about a book I had read, I'm reading or about to read! So I thought, why not start a book club online? What if we all got together and dissected One Hundred Years of Solitude once again or fell in love with Pulitzer Prize-winning author Stacy Schiff's new novel, The Revolutionary Samuel Adams.

Welcome to the Diogenes' Lantern Club.


There are tons of great books I'd love to share with you and because I love to tell you about as many as I can, tons of excellent upcoming must-read books should make your reading list this 2022! More on that at the bottom of the page.


So fellow honest men, women and non-binary, join us as we immerse ourselves into these stories. Remember, you are not obligated to finish a book; there's no deadline or homework. Seriously, we are not your average book club. Did you just finish a chapter and want to share your thoughts? Or there's a zinger you just can't wait to run by us? We are here, and you have a ready-made community for your entertainment, no matter what that entails. Let’s talk about it!


For the avid readers and the perpetually curious out there, get ready to burn the midnight oil.

But have you read ...?

Tochi Onyebuchi returns with his latest book tackling income inequality, gentrification, and racism with Goliath. A mass exodus of the white population to space colonies has left the poor BIPOC population to struggle on an uninhabitable earth. Years later, the still powerful and privileged space colonists have begun to return - some on a mission to gentrify once abandoned neighbourhoods and others as tourists and gawkers of human misery.


A fantastical body of work, Onyebuchi expertly weaves an intriguing and electrifying tale that spans worlds exploring current issues and trajectory of the present human life and conditions: classism, racism, climate crisis and a broken social and justice system.

This Month's Spotlight

Oranges Are Not The Only Fruit

From Jeannette Winterson, the winner of the Whitbread Prize for best first fiction, Oranges are not the only fruit is a coming-out story that renders coming to terms with one's sexuality and religion so beautifully and humanely. The narrator, Jeanette, grappling with whether 'God loves all' is explored brilliantly strikes a chord inside, bolstering and cementing a powerful and indelible lesson: to see, embrace and love your whole self, unconditionally.
